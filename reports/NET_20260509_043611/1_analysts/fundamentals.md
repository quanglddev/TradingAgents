# NET (Cloudflare, Inc.) — Fundamentals Analysis

**Date:** 2026-05-09 | **Ticker:** NET | **Sector:** Software - Infrastructure

---

## 1. Business snapshot
- Cloudflare operates global cloud network for content delivery, security (DDoS, WAF, Zero Trust), and edge compute. Revenue model: subscription-based, tiered plans, expanding enterprise mix.
- FY2025 revenue $2,168M, +29.8% YoY (FY2024: $1,670M). Quarterly run-rate exited at $615M (Q4'25), implying ~$2.46B annualized.
- Revenue split unavailable from tool data (no segment disclosure in get_fundamentals). Inferred driver: large enterprise adoption plus expanding product portfolio (Workers, R2, Zero Trust).
- Headcount-intensive model: R&D $512M (23.6% of rev), S&M $921M (42.5% of rev) in FY2025. Combined opex $1,823M (84.1% of rev).

## 2. Profitability and growth
- **Gross margin declining sequentially every quarter:** 76.4% (Q4'24) → 75.9% → 74.9% → 74.0% → 73.6% (Q4'25). FY2025 annual: 74.5% vs FY2024: 77.3%, a 280bps compression. Infrastructure/bandwidth costs rising faster than revenue.
- **Operating margin:** FY2025 -9.6% (vs -9.3% FY2024). Q4'25: -8.0%, improving from Q2'25 trough of -13.1%. Trend directionally favorable in H2'25 but still deeply negative.
- **Net margin:** FY2025 -4.72% (vs -4.72% FY2024 essentially flat). Interest income ($131.2M FY2025) masks operating losses, a non-operating crutch.
- **EBITDA:** FY2025 +$105.8M (4.9% margin), positive for second year. Q4'25 EBITDA $45.0M (7.3% margin), highest quarter.
- **DuPont decomposition (FY2025):** ROE -8.16% ≈ Net Margin -4.72% × Asset Turnover 0.36× × Equity Multiplier 4.14×. Leverage leg is high (D/E 241%). Asset turnover diluted by $2B debt-raise cash sitting in ST investments. Profitability leg is negative — equity earns nothing.
- **Revenue growth deceleration watch:** QoQ growth: 4.2% (Q1'25), 6.9% (Q2), 9.7% (Q3), 9.3% (Q4). FY2025 annual 29.8% vs FY2024 28.7% — stable, not accelerating.

## 3. Cash flow quality
- **CFO far exceeds NI:** FY2025 CFO $603.1M vs Net Income -$102.3M. CFO/NI gap driven by SBC add-back ($451.5M), deferred revenue growth ($206.4M current + $19.0M non-current), and D&A ($189.7M).
- **FCF positive and growing:** FY2025 $260.6M (12.0% FCF margin), up from $166.9M (10.0%) in FY2024. Q4'25 FCF $99.4M (16.2% margin) — strongest quarter.
- **Capex intensity rising:** FY2025 capex $342.6M (15.8% of revenue) vs FY2024 $213.5M (12.8%). Cloudflare builds own network (gross PPE $1,236M), capex/revenue likely structurally higher than pure SaaS peers.
- **SBC-adjusted FCF deeply negative:** FY2025 FCF $260.6M minus SBC $451.5M = -$190.9M. SBC runs 20.8% of revenue, flat as % over 4 years. True economic dilution not captured in GAAP FCF.
- **Deferred revenue as cash engine:** Current deferred revenue $684.2M at Q4'25, +43.2% YoY. Non-current $41.1M. Combined deferred revenue growth outpacing revenue growth — positive bookings signal.
- **Accruals proxy (CFO - NI gap ex-SBC):** FY2025: $603.1M - (-$102.3M) - $451.5M = $253.9M. Primarily D&A + deferred revenue + working capital. No red flag on manipulative accruals.

## 4. Balance sheet and solvency
- **Total debt ballooned:** $1,463M (FY2024) → $3,518M (FY2025). $2.0B debt issued in FY2025 (per cash flow statement). Current portion $1,291M at Q4'25 (vs $0 in Q4'24), suggesting convertible notes now current or revolver drawn.
- **Liquidity comfortable but structure matters:** Cash + ST investments $4,101M vs total debt $3,518M. Net cash position if counting ST investments: +$583M. However, $1,291M current debt vs $944M cash alone — they need ST investment liquidation or refinancing to meet near-term obligations.
- **Net debt trajectory:** $1,140M (FY2024) → $2,322M (FY2025). Net debt / TTM EBITDA = not meaningful (EBITDA $105.8M, ratio ~22×). Interest coverage (EBIT/Interest): FY2025 -9.6× on GAAP basis; on cash basis, CFO covers interest ~69×.
- **Equity thin relative to debt:** Stockholders' equity $1,459M vs total liabilities $4,577M. D/E ratio 241%. Retained earnings -$1,205M cumulative. Equity propped up by paid-in capital ($2,651M), not retained profits.
- **Dilution steady:** Shares outstanding: 330.1M (FY2022) → 337.5M → 344.9M → 351.9M (FY2025). ~2.0-2.2% annual dilution. No buybacks (zero all periods). SBC is primary driver.
- **Current ratio 1.98** — adequate, but down from 2.86 at FY2024 due to current debt reclassification.
- **No disclosed debt maturity wall** in tool data; the $1,291M current debt is the near-term exposure to monitor.

## 5. Earnings quality red flags
- **Gross margin compression is the #1 flag:** 280bps annual decline, sequential every quarter in FY2025. Not a one-off. If trend continues at ~70bps/quarter, gross margin hits ~71% by Q2'26. Infrastructure cost structure may have negative operating leverage.
- **SBC masks true profitability:** SBC $451.5M exceeds all FCF generated ($260.6M). GAAP net loss understates economic loss when SBC is factored as real compensation cost. Dilution compounds.
- **Interest income props up bottom line:** $131.2M interest income in FY2025 vs $8.8M interest expense. If short-term rates decline (Fed cuts), this tailwind reverses — net interest buffer shrinks, net losses deepen.
- **Debt-raise purpose unclear from data:** $2B raised in FY2025 while holding $1.86B in ST investments at prior year-end. No large acquisitions apparent in cash flow (no business purchase line). Appears to be opportunistic convertible issuance, but puts $1,291M current burden on balance sheet.
- **No Beneish-style manipulation flags:** Receivables growing slower than revenue (DSO improving from 62.7 to 56.7 days). Deferred revenue growing faster than revenue. No large one-offs in income statement. No sudden tax-rate shifts.
- **Piotroski F-Score: 2/9** (CFO positive, CFO > NI). Fails on: net income, ROA trend, leverage increase, current ratio decline, dilution, gross margin decline, asset turnover decline. Consistent with a growth company but quantitatively weak.

## 6. Bull case / Bear case checklists

### Bull case — must remain true:
- Revenue growth holds ≥25% YoY through FY2026 (current ~30%).
- Gross margin stabilizes above 72% (Q4'25: 73.6%; if compression halts).
- Deferred revenue growth stays ≥30% YoY (current +43%).
- FCF margin expands to ≥15% (Q4'25: 16.2%, already there).
- Interest income remains ≥$100M annually (Fed does not cut aggressively).
- $1,291M current debt refinanced without equity dilution.
- SBC as % of revenue begins declining from 20.8%.

### Bear case — breaks if:
- Gross margin falls below 71% (implies ~3 more quarters of compression).
- Revenue growth decelerates below 22% YoY (current 29.8%).
- Deferred revenue growth drops below 20% (signals bookings slowdown).
- Interest income drops below $80M (Fed cuts 200bps+).
- Current debt rollover forces equity raise, accelerating dilution.
- Capex intensity stays ≥16% (limits FCF expansion).
- SBC stays ≥20% of revenue indefinitely, keeping true FCF negative.

## 8. Actionable takeaways and watchlist metrics
- Gross margin erosion is the single largest risk — 280bps annual compression undermines the operating leverage thesis. If unchecked, pathway to GAAP profitability extends indefinitely. [Conf: High]
- SBC at 20.8% of revenue means true economic dilution outpaces FCF generation. SBC-adjusted FCF was -$191M in FY2025. Equity holders are being diluted ~2%/yr with no offset. [Conf: High]
- Deferred revenue growth (+43% YoY) is the strongest positive signal — suggests underlying demand and bookings remain robust despite margin compression. [Conf: High]
- $2B debt raise transformed balance sheet. Net debt $2.3B with $1.3B current — refinancing execution in coming quarters critical. Cash + ST investments provide buffer but deployment matters. [Conf: Med]
- Interest income tailwind ($131M) is rate-sensitive. A 200bps Fed cut would cut ~$60-80M from pre-tax income, widening GAAP losses materially. [Conf: Med]
- Revenue growth at ~30% remains premium-tier for SaaS. Growth durability vs margin trade-off is the key investor debate. [Conf: Med]
- Piotroski 2/9 signals fundamental weakness by classical metrics — but typical for hyper-growth SaaS. Use for context, not as standalone signal. [Conf: Low]

**Watchlist (next quarter triggers):**
1. **Gross margin:** If Q1'26 drops below 73.0% (from 73.6% Q4'25) → compression trend intact, bearish.
2. **Deferred revenue QoQ growth:** If current deferred revenue grows <5% QoQ (vs 14.2% in Q4'25) → bookings deceleration signal.
3. **Revenue growth:** If Q1'26 revenue <$625M (implies <1.7% QoQ) → growth trajectory concerns.
4. **SBC/revenue ratio:** If >21% in Q1'26 (from 20.1% Q4'25) → dilution accelerating.
5. **Current debt:** Monitor for refinancing announcement; any equity-linked raise is dilution-bearish.
6. **Capex/revenue:** If >16% in Q1'26 → FCF margin expansion stalled.
