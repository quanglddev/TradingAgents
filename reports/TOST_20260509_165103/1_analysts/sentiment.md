## 4. Reflexivity and squeeze risk

**Negative reflexive risk present but unconfirmed. Squeeze indicators absent.**

The post-earnings 10.5% drop on a beat is a textbook reflexive signal: good news (EPS +28.6% above consensus) → negative price reaction → bearish narratives amplify → forced or emotional selling → lower price → balance-sheet or growth concerns become self-fulfilling. However, this requires a second leg of selling to confirm; one-day drop alone is not a feedback loop.

**Squeeze indicators not detected in retrieved articles:** No short-interest commentary, no low-float chatter, no gamma/options-flow discussion, no retail meme focal points, no WallStreetBets or Reddit mentions. The BMO $35 PT suggests institutional upside conviction, not a crowded short.

**Contrarian signal:** Coverage tone is not extreme one-sided. Analyst bullishness (Outperform) coexists with negative price momentum — this is tension, not euphoria or despair. Not an extreme sentiment reading. However, if the selloff deepens without new fundamental deterioration, the post-earnings drop pattern could mark a near-term exhaustion point. Flag as watch item, not actionable contrarian entry yet.
