## 4. Reflexivity and squeeze risk

- **Reflexive uptrend elements present:** 21% single-week surge on earnings beat + guidance raise is textbook reflexive catalyst. Rising price → positive coverage → potential retail interest inflow → higher price. Simply Wall St. publishing a "too late?" piece is itself a signal that the move is drawing valuation scrutiny — a second-order reflexive marker.
- **Squeeze indicators: Absent.** No short-interest discussion, no low-float chatter, no gamma/options-flow commentary, no retail-meme focal points in any retrieved article. The move appears fundamentally driven (earnings revision), not squeeze-driven.
- **Contrarian signal:** The "Is It Too Late?" headline after a 21% jump, published by a mainstream financial outlet, is a mild contrarian yellow flag. One-sided bullish post-earnings coverage with a single valuation caution piece suggests sentiment is positive but not at euphoric extreme. Not yet at mean-reversion danger zone, but worth monitoring if bullish coverage intensifies without new fundamental news.
