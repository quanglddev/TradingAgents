## 2. Profitability and growth

- Q1 2026 net interest income USD 693.0m, up 39.0% vs Q1 2025; interest income up 31.1% to USD 1.001b while interest expense up 16.2% to USD 308.0m.
- Margin scale real: pretax margin 18.1% in Q1 2026 vs 10.3% in Q1 2025; net margin 15.2% vs 9.2%. Selling, general and administrative (SG&A) / revenue = 49.8% in FY2025 vs 53.5% in FY2024; general and administrative / revenue = 19.5% vs 23.0%.
- Q1 2026 pretax income USD 199.6m, up 150.1% vs Q1 2025, up 7.7% vs Q4 2025. Net income USD 166.7m down 3.9% vs Q4 2025 only because tax rate rose to 16.4% from 6.4%.
- FY2025 headline net income USD 481.3m, down 3.5% vs FY2024, hides stronger core trend: pretax income USD 525.9m vs USD 233.3m; normalized income USD 488.7m vs USD 452.3m. FY2024 benefited from negative tax provision USD 265.3m and unusual items USD 58.7m.
- DuPont: FY2025 return on equity approx 5.7% = 13.3% net margin × 0.083× asset turnover × 5.11× leverage; FY2024 approx 8.3%. Change driven mostly by margin normalization and slightly lower leverage, partly offset by better turnover. Vendor trailing return on equity 6.6%, return on assets 1.26%.

## 3. Cash flow quality
- Operating cash flow and net income diverge hard: Q1 2026 operating cash flow -USD 2.315b vs net income +USD 166.7m; FY2025 operating cash flow -USD 3.742b vs net income +USD 481.3m.
- Driver = balance-sheet build: change in working capital -USD 2.619b in Q1 2026; -USD 4.438b in FY2025. For SOFI lender-like model, operating cash flow lower-signal than industrials, but gap this large still matters.
- Free cash flow stayed deeply negative: -USD 2.384b in Q1 2026; -USD 3.994b in FY2025. Capital expenditure only USD 68.8m in Q1 2026 and USD 251.1m in FY2025, so outflow not capital-expenditure-led.
- Funding came from financing/balance-sheet inflows, not internal cash: financing cash flow +USD 13.109b in FY2025, including common stock issuance USD 3.186b.
- Raw accrual read ugly: FY2025 operating cash flow / net income = -7.8×; last 5 quarters combined approx -9.3×. Use caution because financial-company cash-flow classification distorts.
- Stock-based compensation material: USD 72.0m in Q1 2026 = 43% of net income and 6.5% of revenue; USD 262.1m in FY2025 = 54% of net income and 7.3% of revenue.

## 4. Balance sheet and solvency
- Stated debt de-risked: total debt USD 1.915b at Q1 2026 vs USD 3.140b at Q1 2025, down 39%; current debt flat USD 486m across last 5 reported quarters, long-term debt USD 1.328b in Q1 2026.
- Liquidity ample versus stated debt: Q1 2026 cash USD 3.401b + other short-term investments USD 3.048b + available-for-sale securities USD 0.183b = ~USD 6.63b, or 3.46× total debt. FY2025 same ratio ~3.91×.
- Equity build strong: common equity USD 10.812b in Q1 2026 vs USD 6.679b in Q1 2025, up 61.9%; retained-earnings deficit improved to -USD 657.5m from -USD 1.234b. Book value/share approx USD 8.44 vs USD 6.05; tangible book/share approx USD 6.89 vs USD 4.18.
- Statement leverage moderate for financial firm: assets/equity approx 4.97× in Q1 2026 vs 5.65× in Q1 2025. Note mismatch: get_fundamentals Debt to Equity 17.724 vs statement total debt/equity approx 0.18×. Definitions differ; do not mix.
- Goodwill + intangibles USD 1.976b in Q1 2026, 18.3% of equity; goodwill flat USD 1.394b since 2023. No visible acquisition build or fresh impairment risk.
- Capital allocation = capital build, not shareholder return: common stock issuance USD 3.186b in FY2025, USD 87.4m in Q1 2026; buybacks trivial; dividends zero.
- Piotroski-style, SOFI passes 3 of 7 verifiable tests: positive net income, lower leverage, better asset turnover. Fails: positive operating cash flow, operating cash flow > net income, dilution, return on assets improvement. Two tests unavailable/low-signal for financials.

## 5. Earnings quality red flags
- Biggest red flag = earnings/cash divergence: Q1 2026 net income +USD 166.7m vs operating cash flow -USD 2.315b; FY2025 +USD 481.3m vs -USD 3.742b. Sector caveat real, magnitude still too large to ignore.
- FY2024 comp base low quality: negative tax provision USD 265.3m plus unusual items USD 58.7m boosted reported net income. Any simple FY2025 vs FY2024 earnings comparison misleads.
- Receivables rose faster than revenue in FY2025: USD 893.5m vs USD 587.5m, up 52%, while revenue rose 38%. Could be benign mix effect for financial firm; still forensic watch item.
- Dilution masks some operating progress: diluted average shares 1.252b in FY2025 vs 1.101b in FY2024, up 13.7%; Q1 2026 diluted average shares 1.378b vs 1.185b in Q1 2025, up 16.2%.
- No major current one-off/impairment problem visible: unusual items only -USD 8.1m in FY2025 and -USD 2.4m in Q1 2026; goodwill stable.

## 6. Bull case / Bear case checklists
- Bull:
  - Revenue stays above USD 1.0b per quarter and above 30% growth vs prior-year quarter; net interest income stays above 25% growth vs prior-year quarter.
  - Pretax margin holds at or above 18%; net margin holds at or above 15%.
  - Interest income growth continues to outrun interest expense growth by more than 10 percentage points.
  - Liquidity stays above 3.0× stated debt; total debt stays at or below USD 2.0b; equity stays above USD 10.5b.
  - Dilution slows: period-end shares outstanding grow less than 2% quarter/quarter and stock-based compensation stays below 7% of revenue.
  - Book value/share stays above ~USD 8.40; tangible book/share stays above ~USD 6.80.

- Bear:
  - Revenue growth falls below 20% vs prior-year quarter or revenue falls back below USD 1.0b per quarter.
  - Pretax margin falls below 15% or net margin below 12%.
  - Another quarter with operating cash flow worse than -USD 2.0b and working-capital use worse than -USD 2.0b while net income stays positive.
  - New common stock issuance above USD 250m in quarter or period-end shares outstanding above 1.35b.
  - Liquidity / stated debt falls below 2.0× or total debt rises above USD 2.5b.
  - Receivables growth stays more than 10 percentage points above revenue growth, or tax benefits / unusual items again become major earnings driver.
