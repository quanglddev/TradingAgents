## 5. Earnings quality red flags

### Flag 1: Massive SBC dilutes true economic earnings
SBC FY2025: $684.0M = 42.1% of net income. SBC as % of revenue declining (24.1% FY2024 → 15.3% FY2025), but absolute dollars remain large. Real shareholder dilution via option exercises: $129.1M proceeds, but shares outstanding +2.24%. [Conf: High]

### Flag 2: Unsustainably low effective tax rate
FY2025 tax provision: $22.7M on $1,657.4M pretax income = 1.4% effective rate. NOL carryforwards (retained earnings –$3,562M) being consumed. As NOLs deplete, rate normalizes to 21%+ federal; net income mechanically drops 20%+ all else equal. [Conf: High]

### Flag 3: Deferred revenue growth lagging revenue
Combined deferred revenue +44.9% YoY vs revenue +70.0%. Suggests revenue recognition outpacing new bookings growth. If trend continues, indicates decelerating contracted backlog. [Conf: Med]

### Flag 4: Receivables growth outpacing revenue
AR Q4 2025: $1,042.1M, +81.2% YoY vs revenue +70.0% YoY. DSO ~67 days vs ~63 days a year ago. Not yet alarming but bears monitoring in a high-growth company where aggressive recognition can mask slowdown. [Conf: Med]

### Flag 5: Low GAAP tax rate masks true earnings power for valuation
P/E of 154.8× (TTM, per fundamentals) is on 1.4% tax rate. On normalized 21% rate, TTM net income ≈ $1,800M (roughly $2,281M × 0.79), P/E ≈ 183×. Market pricing perfection. [Conf: Med]

## 6. Bull case / Bear case checklists

### Bull case — must remain true:
1. Revenue growth sustains >40% YoY through FY2026. Q4 2025 was +70%; any deceleration below 40% threatens the multiple.
2. Gross margin stays >80%. Q4 2025 at 84.6% — if mix shifts toward lower-margin commercial or hosting costs rise, margin compression bites hard on net income.
3. Operating margin continues expanding (Q4 2025: 40.9%). Any reversal signals operating deleverage on slowing growth.
4. CFO/NI ratio stays >1.0×. SBC remains non-cash addback that sustains reported FCF. If options cash-settle or SBC declines, CFO weakens.
5. NOLs remain adequate to keep effective tax rate <5% through at least FY2026. Tax normalization is a known headwind; timing matters.
6. Deferred revenue growth re-accelerates to >50% YoY or at minimum stabilizes at current gap. Further divergence implies bookings deceleration.

### Bear case — what breaks:
1. Revenue growth drops below 30% YoY. Operating expense growth (currently controlled at +25.8% in Q4) would overtake revenue, crushing margins.
2. AR/Revenue ratio spikes above 20% (currently 18.5%); DSO crosses 75 days. Signals collection issues or channel-stuffing.
3. SBC % of revenue stops declining, stays >15%. Dilution continues eating 2%+ of shares annually, eroding per-share metrics.
4. Tax rate jumps above 10% effective — NOLs exhausted faster than modeled. Net income drops 10-20% overnight.
5. Net cash position deployed in large, questionable M&A. $6.9B net cash is both a cushion and a risk if management overpays for growth.
