## 1. Business snapshot
- DOC = Healthpeak Properties, Inc.; real estate investment trust (REIT) in healthcare facilities; market cap USD 13.54b; dividend yield 6.21% on 2026-05-09. (get_fundamentals)
- 2025-12-31 asset base heavy: investment properties USD 16.099b, long-term equity investments USD 802.6m, total assets USD 20.336b. (get_balance_sheet annual)
- Revenue engine mostly recurring property operations: 2025 full year operating revenue USD 2.823b; 2025 quarters stayed in USD 694.3m-719.4m range. (get_income_statement annual/quarterly)
- Reported profit not clean proxy for core economics: 2025 full year depreciation/amortization about USD 1.0b, gain on sale of security USD 69.5m, equity-interest loss USD 174.0m. Cash metrics matter more. (get_income_statement annual; get_cashflow annual)

## 2. Profitability and growth
- 2025 full year revenue USD 2.823b, +4.5% year over year vs 2024 full year USD 2.700b; Q4 2025 revenue USD 719.4m, +1.9% vs Q3 2025 and +3.1% vs Q4 2024 USD 698.0m. (get_income_statement annual/quarterly)
- 2025 gross profit USD 1.693b; gross margin 60.0% vs 60.2% in 2024. Quarterly gross margin held 58.6%-61.1% through 2025. No clear property-level margin break. (get_income_statement annual/quarterly)
- 2025 operating income USD 544.1m; operating margin 19.3% vs 17.4% in 2024. Q4 2025 operating margin 20.3% vs 17.6% in Q4 2024. Core operating leverage improved. (get_income_statement annual/quarterly)
- Reported bottom line weak: 2025 full year common net income USD 70.5m, net margin 2.5%, diluted earnings per share USD 0.10; 2024 full year common net income USD 242.4m, net margin 9.0%, diluted earnings per share USD 0.36. Vendor normalized income fell to USD 28.0m from USD 220.5m. (get_income_statement annual)
- Below-operating-line drag large: 2025 interest expense USD 305.2m, +8.8% year over year; earnings from equity interest net of tax -USD 174.0m vs -USD 1.5m in 2024. Operating improvement did not reach common shareholders. (get_income_statement annual)
- DuPont read on common equity: 2025 return on equity about 0.9% = 2.5% net margin × 0.14× asset turnover × 2.5× leverage, down from about 3.3% in 2024. Damage came from margin collapse; leverage slightly higher, turnover slightly lower. (derived from get_income_statement annual; get_balance_sheet annual)

## 3. Cash flow quality
- Operating cash flow (CFO) 2025 full year USD 1.252b, +17.0% year over year vs USD 1.070b in 2024; all 2025 quarters positive: USD 279.4m, USD 363.5m, USD 315.0m, USD 294.1m. (get_cashflow annual/quarterly)
- Cash conversion strong: 2025 CFO USD 1.252b vs common net income USD 70.5m; CFO to earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization (EBITDA) 0.76× vs 0.66× in 2024. Gap mostly structural: depreciation/amortization about USD 1.0b and noncash equity losses large. (get_cashflow annual; get_income_statement annual)
- Working-capital support modest, not whole story: 2025 working-capital benefit USD 105.7m; receivables change -USD 16.3m; payables change -USD 25.3m. No giant period-end unwind propping cash. (get_cashflow annual)
- Vendor free cash flow equals CFO in every shown quarter and year: USD 1.252b in 2025, same as CFO. Maintenance capex line absent; true free cash flow margin and capex intensity not computable from tool output. (get_cashflow annual/quarterly)
- Property investment spend heavy: 2025 gross property purchases USD 1.250b, property sales USD 337.7m, net property spend USD 912.6m; dividends USD 849.1m. Dividend covered by CFO 1.47×, but dividend plus net property spend exceeded CFO by about USD 509.8m. (get_cashflow annual)

## 4. Balance sheet and solvency
- 2025-12-31 total debt USD 10.144b; vendor net debt USD 9.380b; unrestricted cash USD 467.5m, restricted cash USD 70.2m. Debt rose from USD 9.023b total and USD 8.596b net at 2024-12-31. (get_balance_sheet annual/quarterly)
- Leverage worse: net debt to EBITDA about 5.7× in 2025 vs about 5.3× in 2024; total debt to stockholders' equity 1.35× vs 1.07×. Stockholders' equity fell to USD 7.500b from USD 8.401b. (derived from get_balance_sheet annual; get_income_statement annual)
- Liquidity tighter: current debt jumped more than 7× to USD 1.079b from USD 150m; current liabilities USD 1.809b vs current assets USD 1.303b; working capital -USD 506.6m vs +USD 49.5m. Direct balance-sheet current ratio about 0.72×. (get_balance_sheet annual/quarterly)
- Cash cushion thin versus near-term debt: unrestricted cash covered only 43% of current debt; cash plus restricted cash covered about 50%. (derived from get_balance_sheet annual/quarterly)
- Interest burden rising: 2025 EBITDA covered interest expense about 5.4× vs 5.7× in 2024, 6.3× in 2023, 8.0× in 2022. Not distress now; trend negative. (derived from get_income_statement annual)
- Refinancing churn high but market access open: 2025 debt issuance USD 13.108b, debt repayment USD 11.999b. Company refinancing and adding leverage, not de-risking. (get_cashflow annual)
- Capital allocation mixed: 2025 buybacks USD 97.1m cut year-end shares to 695.0m from 699.5m (-0.6%), but diluted average shares still rose to 696.0m from 676.2m (+2.9%); year-end shares still 27.0% above 2023 year-end 547.2m. (get_cashflow annual; get_balance_sheet annual; get_income_statement annual)
- Piotroski-style read 4/9 pass: pass on positive net income, positive CFO, CFO above net income, lower year-end share count; fail on return on assets, leverage, current ratio, gross margin, asset turnover. (derived from annual statements)

## 5. Earnings quality red flags
- 2025 full year gain on sale of security USD 69.5m almost equaled common net income USD 70.5m; 2024 also carried USD 178.7m of such gains. Pattern = bottom-line reliance on sale gains. (get_income_statement annual)
- Q4 2025 headline net income USD 114.0m included gain on sale of security USD 56.4m and total unusual items USD 49.8m; normalized income USD 66.6m. Reported quarter stronger than recurring quarter. (get_income_statement quarterly)
- Q3 2025 net loss USD 117.1m came with positive CFO USD 315.0m because equity-interest loss was -USD 176.3m and cash flow added back noncash losses. Earnings noisy; cash steadier. (get_income_statement quarterly; get_cashflow quarterly)
- Cross-statement joint-venture flag: long-term equity investments fell 14.3% to USD 802.6m from USD 936.8m while 2025 equity-interest result was -USD 174.0m. Off-balance-sheet exposure hurt headline earnings. (get_balance_sheet annual; get_income_statement annual)
- No classic receivables or gross-margin forensic flag seen: accounts receivable USD 78.3m vs USD 76.8m in 2024 while revenue grew 4.5%; gross margin stayed near 60%. (get_balance_sheet annual; get_income_statement annual)

## 6. Bull case / Bear case checklists
- Bull:
  - Quarterly revenue stays at least USD 700m; annual revenue growth stays at least 3%.
  - Gross margin holds 59%-61%; operating margin stays at least 19%.
  - Quarterly CFO stays at least USD 280m; annual CFO at least USD 1.20b; cash dividend at or below 70% of CFO.
  - Net debt to EBITDA stays at or below 5.7×; EBITDA interest coverage at least 5.0×.
  - Current debt moves back below USD 800m or liquidity improves enough to lift direct current ratio above 0.9×.
  - Equity-interest losses normalize; no repeat of more than USD 100m full-year drag.
- Bear:
  - Quarterly revenue falls below USD 690m or annual growth stalls to 0% or worse.
  - Gross margin falls below 58.5% or operating margin below 18%.
  - Quarterly CFO falls below USD 250m or annual CFO below USD 1.10b; dividend coverage drops below 1.30×.
  - Net debt to EBITDA rises above 6.0× or EBITDA interest coverage falls below 5.0×.
  - Current debt stays above USD 1.0b with direct current ratio still below 0.8×.
  - Gains/unusual items again supply more than 50% of net income, or share count starts rising more than 1% year over year while leverage still climbs.

## 8. Actionable takeaways and watchlist metrics
- DOC core operating engine steady, not accelerating: 2025 full year revenue USD 2.823b (+4.5%), gross margin 60.0%, operating margin 19.3%; 2025 quarters stayed near USD 700m revenue. [Conf: High]
- DOC reported earnings quality weak: 2025 full year gain on sale of security USD 69.5m nearly matched common net income USD 70.5m; normalized income only USD 28.0m; Q4 2025 also flattered by USD 56.4m security gains. [Conf: High]
- DOC cash generation stronger than reported earnings: 2025 full year CFO USD 1.252b, positive in every 2025 quarter, dividend covered 1.47×; depreciation/amortization near USD 1.0b explains much gap. [Conf: High]
- DOC balance-sheet risk rising: net debt USD 9.380b, net debt to EBITDA 5.7×, current debt USD 1.079b, working capital -USD 506.6m, direct current ratio about 0.72×. Bear thesis should press refinancing and liquidity, not receivables. [Conf: High]
- DOC dividend fine on CFO alone, not self-funded after growth spend: 2025 dividend plus net property spend exceeded CFO by about USD 509.8m, leaving debt markets and asset recycling important. [Conf: Med]
- DOC per-share scale progress weak: revenue per diluted average share rose only to about USD 4.05 in 2025 from about USD 3.98 in 2023, despite total revenue up 29%. Prior dilution absorbed most scale gain. [Conf: Med]

- Watchlist:
  - Quarterly revenue: keep at least USD 700m; alert if below USD 690m.
  - Operating margin: keep at least 19%; alert if below 18%.
  - Quarterly CFO: keep at least USD 280m; alert if below USD 250m.
  - Net debt to EBITDA: keep at or below 5.7×; alert if above 6.0×.
  - Direct current ratio / current debt: improve to above 0.9× or current debt below USD 800m; alert if current ratio stays below 0.8× with current debt above USD 1.0b.
  - Equity-interest plus security-gain noise: keep combined absolute swing below USD 50m per quarter; alert if above USD 75m.
