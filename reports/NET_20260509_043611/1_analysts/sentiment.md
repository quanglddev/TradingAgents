## 4. Reflexivity and squeeze risk

**Reflexive downtrend signals present, but contrarian bullish defense active.**

- **Pre-earnings reflexivity:** 43% rally since February → bullish coverage → high expectations → crowded positioning → sharp negative reaction when guidance missed. Classic reflexive peak pattern.
- **Current risk — reflexive downtrend:** Falling stock → bearish coverage (layoff headlines amplify) → potential forced selling / stop-outs → lower price. Jefferies' "near-term growth could be hurt" by layoffs provides fundamental validation loop.
- **Squeeze indicators: absent.** No article mentions high short interest, low float, retail-meme activity, gamma squeezes, or options-driven positioning. This is an institutional AI/thematic story, not a meme squeeze.
- **Contrarian signal: active.** Piper Sandler hiking PT to $250 during 24% selloff is explicit "buy the dip" call. If more analysts follow, could arrest reflexive downtrend. Moneywise's generous-severance angle also softens negative narrative.
- **AI-demand bull case as reflexive stabilizer:** If AI revenue numbers accelerate in Q2, fundamental validation could flip the narrative back bullish — layoffs then reframed as margin-expansion genius rather than distress.

**Assessment:** Not a squeeze setup. High-conviction selloff driven by guidance + layoff shock. Risk of further downside if Q2 execution stumbles, but analyst defense and AI-demand data provide potential floor.
