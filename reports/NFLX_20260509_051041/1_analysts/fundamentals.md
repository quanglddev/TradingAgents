## 5. Earnings quality red flags
- **Q1 FY26 interest income anomaly**: Interest income jumped to $2.85B vs ~$45M in Q4 FY25. This singlehandedly boosted pretax income $2.8B and net income ~$2.3B after tax. Possible FX gain or reclassification; if non-recurring, core EPS would be ~$0.70 instead of $1.23. **High-priority item to clarify**.
- **Gross margin volatility**: Q1 FY26 51.9% vs Q4 FY25 45.9% vs Q1 FY25 50.1%. Swing partly due to content amortization timing and revenue seasonality; watch for consistency.
- **Receivables up**: AR $2.00B (Q1 FY26) vs $2.03B (Q4 FY25) flat, but vs $1.45B (Q1 FY25) → +38% YoY while revenue +16%. DSO rising—monitor. Could reflect ad-tier receivables with longer collection cycles.
- **Accrued expenses growth**: Accrued expenses $4.44B (Q1 FY26) vs $2.36B (Q1 FY25) → +88% YoY. Partly tied to content/legal obligations; large swing.

No clear Beneish-style manipulation signals (CFO ≈ NI, no asset quality deterioration, LVGI improving), but the interest income spike warrants diligence.

## 6. Bull case / Bear case checklists

### Bull case — conditions that must hold
1. Interest income anomaly is recurring or at least non-dilutive (clarify source).
2. Revenue growth stays ≥ 10% YoY (Q1 FY26: +16%).
3. Operating margin ≥ 28% (current 32%; guidance often 28% floor).
4. FCF margin ≥ 18% (current 21% FY25) sustaining buyback cadence.
5. Net debt remains ≤ 0.5× EBITDA.
6. Subscriber net adds remain positive (not directly in data—external confirmation needed).

### Bear case — conditions that would break thesis
1. Interest income one-off; core NI closer to $3B for Q1 → FY26E EPS downgrades.
2. Content cost inflation outpaces ARPU growth; gross margin < 45%.
3. Advertising tier fails to scale; growth deceleration to < 8%.
4. Receivables/revenue divergence widens > 1.5× revenue growth for 2+ quarters → collection risk.
5. Leverage reversal (acquisitions or content bidding wars push net debt/EBITDA > 2×).
